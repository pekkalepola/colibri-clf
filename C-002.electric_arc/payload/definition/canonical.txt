Concept: C-002.electric_arc
Kind: phenomenon

Definition:
An electric arc is a self-sustaining electrical discharge through a gas or
plasma medium. It is characterized by a luminous conducting plasma channel
between two points, with intense light emission spanning the ultraviolet and
visible spectrum, extreme localized heat, a distinctive acoustic signature,
ozone and nitrogen oxide formation, and broadband electromagnetic radiation
including radiofrequency interference.

This concept does NOT define:
- Voltage or current values associated with arc initiation or sustaining
- Arc duration, energy, or power
- Electrode material or geometry
- Protective or corrective actions
- Detection methods or algorithms

Semantic scope:
Applicable to electrical discharges in gaseous media, including ambient air,
inert gases, and partial vacuum. The defining characteristic is the plasma
channel — a conducting, luminous column sustained by the discharge itself.
