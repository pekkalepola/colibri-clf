Concept: C-004.abnormal_vibration
Kind: phenomenon

Definition:
Abnormal vibration is a mechanical phenomenon in which a system or component
exhibits oscillatory motion of unusual character. It is characterized by
elevated vibration amplitude, irregular or atypical spectral content, or
structural resonances not present during expected operation. The phenomenon
is perceivable as shaking, knocking, or trembling through direct contact,
as audible sound, and as visible oscillatory displacement of the system.

This concept does NOT define:
- Amplitude thresholds or frequency values that constitute abnormality
- The source or origin of the vibration (imbalance, misalignment, bearing fault, etc.)
- Severity classification or remaining useful life
- Detection methods or algorithms

Semantic scope:
Applicable to mechanical systems in operation, including rotating machinery,
reciprocating machines, structures under dynamic load, and fluid-mechanical
systems. The phenomenon is defined by the character of the oscillatory motion,
not by its origin.
