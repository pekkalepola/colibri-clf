Concept: C-003.partial_discharge
Kind: phenomenon

Definition:
A partial discharge is a localized electrical discharge within or on the surface
of an insulating medium. It is characterized by repetitive, sub-microsecond
current pulses confined to a region that does not completely bridge the gap
between conductors. The discharge is accompanied by broadband electromagnetic
pulse emission in the UHF range, ultrasonic acoustic emission, and the formation
of ozone and other chemical byproducts. The phenomenon occurs in the presence
of localized electric field intensification within the insulating medium.

This concept does NOT define:
- Voltage levels or field strengths associated with discharge onset
- Pulse repetition rate or apparent charge magnitude
- Insulation material type or geometry
- Classification into internal, surface, or corona sub-types
- Severity or remaining lifetime of the insulating medium
- Detection methods or algorithms

Semantic scope:
Applicable to solid, liquid, and gaseous insulating media in the presence of
an electric field. The defining characteristic is the spatial locality of the
discharge — it does not bridge the full insulation gap.
